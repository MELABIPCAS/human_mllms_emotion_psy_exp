### Table 5: Structured Prompts for MLLM Evaluation (Task I)

| Stage                  | Prompt Content                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Q1: Congruence         | Do you think the facial expression depicted in the presented image is emotionally congruent? Please respond with 'yes' or 'no' (if you are unable to make a definitive judgment, provide the answer you believe is most likely) |
| Q2: Congruence rating  | Based on your answer in Q1, rate the strength of your judgment on a 5-point scale (1–5). Please output one integer from 1 to 5.<br>• If you answered CONGRUENT in Q1: 1 = slightly congruent, 2 = somewhat congruent, 3 = moderately congruent, 4 = very congruent, 5 = highly congruent.<br>• If you answered INCONGRUENT in Q1: 1 = slightly incongruent, 2 = somewhat incongruent, 3 = moderately incongruent, 4 = very incongruent, 5 = highly incongruent. |
| Q3: Valence            | Do you consider the facial expression in the image to reflect a positive or a negative emotion? Please respond with 'positive emotion' or 'negative emotion' (if you are unable to make a definitive judgment, provide the answer you believe is most likely) |
| Q4: Category           | Which emotion do you think is expressed in the image—happiness, surprise, sadness, disgust, fear, or anger? Please respond with one of these six terms (if you are unable to make a definitive judgment, provide the answer you believe is most likely) |
| Q5: Attention          | Which region of the face did you rely on when judging the emotion expressed in the image? Please respond with 'upper face' or 'lower face' (if you are unable to make a definitive judgment, provide the answer you believe is most likely) |
